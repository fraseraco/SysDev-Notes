# üìà Greenfield Projects & Strategic Visibility

## üéØ Objective

Identify, design, and deliver new internal tools or process improvements that:
- increase efficiency 
- reduce manual effort 
- elevate IT‚Äôs visibility as a **strategic partner** rather than an operational cost.  

Projects should align with:
- business goals
- showcase technical competence
- produce measurable ROI or user impact.

---

## üìç Scope
**Includes**
- Proposals for new internal applications or automation systems  
- Process improvement and data-driven initiatives  
- Integrations that streamline workflows across departments  
- Low-code or full-stack tools that demonstrate business value  
- Metrics and case studies for executive visibility

**Excludes**
- Refactoring or maintenance of legacy systems (see [[Refactor & Rebuild Planning]])  
- Routine IT support (see [[IT Operations & Support (Overview)]])

---

## üß≠ Strategy
- Target **pain points with broad visibility** (e.g., manual data entry, disjointed workflows).  
- Prioritize **projects that free up employee time** or **improve decision-making** through better data.  
- Communicate wins effectively ‚Äî short demos, before/after metrics, and visible improvements.  
- Leverage these projects to make the department **budget-positive** in perception and influence.

## üí° Project Ideation Framework
| Criterion | Description |
|------------|--------------|
| **Impact** | How many people or departments benefit |
| **Visibility** | Likelihood executives or end users will notice the change |
| **Feasibility** | Development effort, technical complexity, dependencies |
| **Longevity** | Lifespan and maintainability |
| **Synergy** | Alignment with rebuild and long-term IT strategy |

Use these as scoring axes in `/docs/greenfield-project-matrix.xlsx`.

## üöÄ Project Lifecycle (per-project template)
1. **Proposal (Idea ‚Üí RFC)**  
   - Define problem, business case, scope, timeline, and measurable outcomes.  
2. **Design**  
   - Diagram architecture; reuse components from rebuild stack where possible.  
3. **Prototype**  
   - Timeboxed proof of concept (‚â§2 weeks).  
4. **Pilot**  
   - Limited release to one department; collect feedback & metrics.  
5. **Launch**  
   - Deploy broadly with documentation and announcement.  
6. **Post-Launch Review**  
   - Measure impact, gather testimonials, and present ROI metrics.
